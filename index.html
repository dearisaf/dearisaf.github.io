About Me

With over a decade of experience in the fashion industry, I have carved out my career by blending the rich traditions of Indonesian textiles with modern, sustainable design practices. My work seeks to bridge the gap between contemporary fashion and Indonesia's diverse cultural heritage, creating pieces that celebrate both. Drawing inspiration from the timeless beauty of batik, ikat, and tenun, I strive to create designs that are not only stylish but also socially and environmentally responsible.

I am a proud graduate of State University of Surabaya, where I refined my skills in fashion design and garment technology. After completing my studies, I launched my own label, "Karya Nusantara", based in Surabaya, focusing on elevating traditional Indonesian fabrics and craftsmanship. My label has gained recognition locally, participating in fashion shows like Surabaya Fashion Parade and Indonesia Fashion Week in Jakarta, where I’ve showcased collections that blend modern silhouettes with Indonesia’s rich textile history.

Throughout my career, I’ve had the privilege of collaborating with notable Indonesian brands like Batik Keris and Matahari Department Store, where I contributed to their sustainable fashion initiatives, integrating eco-friendly materials and ethical production practices. I’ve also had the opportunity to design for high-profile events, including dressing influencers and celebrities for Jakarta Fashion Week and local awards shows.

My work has been featured in leading fashion publications such as Elle Indonesia and Harper's Bazaar Indonesia, where I’ve been recognized for my innovative approach to modernizing traditional textiles. Moving forward, I am committed to building a brand that not only highlights Indonesia's fashion legacy but also addresses global sustainability challenges in the fashion industry.
